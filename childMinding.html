CHILD MINDING

What is Child Minding?

Child mending is a holistic, trauma-informed approach to nurturing and supporting children who have experienced adversity, trauma, neglect, or other challenges that have disrupted their healthy development. It moves beyond basic care to actively focus on repairing emotional, psychological, and social wounds.

Who Practices Child Minding?
  1. Foster and Adoptive Parents
  2. Social Workers and Therapists (Play therapists, art therapists)
  3. Teachers and School Counselors in trauma-informed schools
  4. Youth Program Leaders in community centers
